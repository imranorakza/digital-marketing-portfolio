03 — Philosophy

Google Ads is not a list of campaigns. It's a system.

Most accounts I've worked on over the years were a collection of disconnected campaigns — good keywords, weak landing pages, no tracking discipline, or the opposite. The accounts that actually produce qualified leads are the ones where every stage feeds the next one. That's the belief my approach is built on.

I don't just build Google Ads campaigns. I build lead generation systems, with Google Ads as one part of the process.

04 — Framework

The account architecture I build on every engagement

This is the structure I take into every new account, then adapt to the business. Each layer exists to feed clean data and clear intent to the layer below it.

LAYER 01 — ACCOUNT

One account for billing, shared assets and conversion tracking. Negative keywords sit at account level or campaign level, depending on what needs to stay separate.

LAYER 02 — CONVERSION TRACKING

GA4 and Google Tag Manager set up, Enhanced Conversions enabled where possible, primary conversions defined. This goes in before campaigns launch — Smart Bidding is only as good as the data it's given.

LAYER 03 — CAMPAIGNS

Few, consolidated campaigns organised by product line and goal — not by every possible segment. Consolidation is what lets Smart Bidding actually learn.

LAYER 04 — THEME-BASED AD GROUPS

Groups of closely related keywords under one theme — one clear buying intent per group, enough volume for the data to mean something.

LAYER 05 — RSAS & ASSETS

A wide, genuinely different set of headlines and descriptions per ad group, with light, deliberate pinning — never a rebuilt static ad.

LAYER 06 — KEYWORDS

Start on Phrase Match for control, then let proven, profitable search terms graduate to Broad Match once Smart Bidding has data to work with.

LAYER 07 — DEDICATED LANDING PAGES

Where it makes sense, every product line gets its own page — its own message, proof and form, rather than a campaign pointing at a generic homepage.

LAYER 08 — OPTIMIZATION

A recurring loop across search terms, negatives, ad assets, landing pages and bids — not a one-time setup.

LAYER 09 — QUALIFIED LEADS

The business outcome that matters most. Everything above this layer exists to improve both the quantity and quality of those leads.

05 — Campaign Structure

Fewer, stronger campaigns outperform dozens of small ones

Google's own guidance and my own account audits generally agree: campaigns split too finely usually don't give Smart Bidding enough conversion data to work with. As a general benchmark, I look for campaigns that can reach something like 30+ conversions a month where the budget allows it.

SEPARATE CAMPAIGNS WHEN

Brand vs Non-brand · Countries · Languages · Major product categories · Search vs Performance Max · Lead generation vs Ecommerce.

DON'T SEPARATE FOR

Devices · Keyword match types · Minor keyword variations · Tiny budgets · Too many campaigns generally.

In practice, I run one campaign per major product line, each with its own landing page, messaging, conversion tracking and reporting. Here's how that looks for the three campaigns used as the example throughout this page — ERP Software, Property Management and CRM.

06 — Keywords, Bidding & Ad Copy

Start narrow, prove it with data, then let the account scale

KEYWORDS

I normally start with Phrase Match, usually 5–15 tightly themed keywords per ad group. As the account collects data, I remove poor performers and add new high-intent terms from the Search Terms report. Once there's enough conversion data, I expand into Broad Match with Smart Bidding.

BIDDING

Every new campaign starts on Maximize Conversions, collecting real data without a forced target too early. Once a campaign has built up roughly 30+ quality conversions and performance has stabilised, I'll consider moving it to Target CPA — only if business goals call for tighter cost control.

AD COPY

Google gives you up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions per RSA — the biggest mistake I still see is writing 15 versions of the same sentence. I use the full asset set, each highlighting a genuinely different angle, and pin sparingly, never solo.

07 — Landing Pages, Message & SEO

One product, one page — and the same message everywhere it shows up

Campaigns of mine typically don't send traffic to a general homepage. Where it makes sense, every major product gets its own page, built around one action — its own message, proof and form. When the page matches the exact keyword and ad the visitor clicked, they don't have to re-orient themselves.

This is one of the things I do differently: when an RSA headline consistently wins in Google Ads, I don't let that insight sit inside the ad account — it can become the landing page headline, the meta title, the meta description, part of the page content, and often an FAQ answer.

Winning Google Ads messages don't stay in the ad account. I turn proven headlines and offers into website content, so SEO builds on what's already been tested with real buyers. Using this loop, I helped achieve page-1 rankings for core product keywords across ERP, CRM, accounting and real estate/property software, while the ad account kept improving in parallel.

08 — Trust & Conversion Tracking

Conversions go up when confidence goes up first

A qualified visitor still won't fill out a form if the page doesn't look credible. I treat trust as a conversion lever, not a design afterthought — industry logos, testimonials, Google reviews, fast loading pages and clear security signals all sit on the landing page for a reason.

02 — The Framework

An 8-step system, revisited every quarter

Not a one-time optimization pass — I run this sequence on every site, then come back quarterly to re-check CTR, rankings and indexation and adjust.

Scope: SEO ownership across UAE, India and KSA, plus technical SEO maintenance across 8 global websites.

STEP 1

Search Intent

Every keyword mapped to what someone is actually trying to do — research, compare or buy — so pages target intent, not just search volume.

STEP 2

Indexation Audit

One of the first things I check is whether Google is indexing the pages that actually matter. I index missing pages and remove unnecessary or dummy pages before moving on to content improvements.

STEP 3

Technical SEO

I check schema, canonical tags, robots directives, clean URLs and crawl health, working with developers where needed. Technical SEO comes before content improvements.

STEP 4

On-Page Foundation

I rewrite titles, meta descriptions and H1s around high-intent keywords, often using messaging that's already proven to convert in Google Ads.

STEP 5

Content Hub & Internal Linking

Content isn't written just to fill a blog. Every guide or comparison targets a search intent, supports a product page and strengthens internal linking.

STEP 6

AI Visibility

I structure pages with schema, clear content and consistent messaging so they're easier for Google and AI assistants like Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity to understand.

STEP 7

Measurement & Quarterly Review

I track form submits, calls, WhatsApp and email clicks through GA4 and GTM, and use Microsoft Clarity to understand user behaviour. I review Search Console quarterly to refine titles and meta descriptions.

STEP 8

Continuous Optimization

SEO is never finished. I review rankings, indexation and content regularly because search behaviour keeps changing.

My approach to SEO goes beyond Google rankings. I focus on making a business visible wherever potential customers search — from Google Search and AI Overviews to ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Google Business Profile.

04 — SEO + Google Ads

Organic data sharpens paid targeting

I use SEO to sharpen paid targeting, not just to chase rankings on its own. Search-term and ranking data shows me which keywords people actually use and where intent is strongest — and that feeds straight back into keyword targeting and ad copy in the paid account.

STEP 1

Search terms reveal real intent

GSC query data shows the exact phrases buyers use — often different from the keywords being bid on.

STEP 2

Rankings show content gaps

Pages ranking just outside page 1 flag topics worth a dedicated landing page or ad group.

STEP 3

Findings feed the next Ads round

New keyword and messaging ideas from organic data go straight into the next Google Ads iteration.
